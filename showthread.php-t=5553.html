TOEFL Practice Test
1. Do you have _____ to do this afternoon? If not, I'd like to take you to a movie.
a) many work
b) much work
c) many works
d) much works
2. We _____ that concert.
a) were disappointed by
b) were disappointed of
c) were disappointing
d) were disappointing in

3. Mr. Johnson has lived here _____ ten years.
a) for
b) during
c) since
d) while

4. The fact ________ money orders can usually be easily cashed has made them a popular form of payment.
a)of
b)that
c)is that
d)which is

5. Gifted though he was by remarkable natural musical talent, _________ to have had little knack for the necessities of social life at court.
a)Mozart, who seemed
b)Mozart seemed
c)it was Mozart that seeming
d)Mozart, seeming

6. __________ they are widely perceived as gentle creatures, hippopotamuses are reponsible for more human deaths in Africa than any other animal.
a)Despite of
b)Even though
c)In spite of
d)Nonetheless

Directions: In question 7, each sentence has four underlined words or phrases. The four underlined parts of the sentence are marked A, B, C, and D. Identify the one underlined word or phrase that must be changed in order for the sentence to be correct.

7. Guppies are sometimes call(A) rainbow fish(B) because(C) of the male's bright(D) colors.
a)A
b)B
c)C
d)D

Level Test-1 (Pre-Intermediate)
1. Richard ----- at 7 o’clock p.m. just as we ---- to have our dinner
A) arrived / were starting
B) has arrived / started
C) would arrive / have started
D) is arriving / would be starting
E) was arriving / will start
2. I always ----- asleep as soon as I get into bed.
A) fall
B) go
C) get
D) fail
E) reach
3. I don’t mind ----- on my own but I’d love ------ people around me.
A) being / to see
B) to be / to see
C) being / seeing
D) having been / seeing
E) to be / to have seen
4. They had three children, ----- was a boy and ----- were girls.
A) one / the others
B) other / another
C) he / they
D) one / all
E) the first / the other
5. His novels have been ----- praised for the superb use of colloquial language.
A) rapidly
B) strangely
C) suddenly
D) safely
E) widely
6. George is very ----- . He has failed his driving test.
A) logical
B) upset
C) reasonable
D) happy
E) punctual
7. She seems ----- to take part in the quiz programme.
A) reluctant
B) hard-working
C) selfish
D) loyal
E) delicate
8. What were Ali and Alice ----- about when the teacher told them to come in?
A) waiting
B) saying
C) discussing
D) talking
E) referring
9. The Namit Desert in Angola is ----- desert in the world.
A) the old
B) as old as
C) oldest
D) older
E) the oldest
10. I ----- there for 5 years when I was transferred.
A) worked
B) was working
C) am working
D) have been working
E) had been working

Level Test-1 (Intermediate)
1. Starring ...... actors in the country didn't prevent the film from being a failure.
A) so well
B) as good
C) better
D) too well
E) the best
2. Recently, it....... that the human race ....... in Africa.
A) was discovered/has been originating
B) is discovered/was originating
C) has been discovered/originated
D) had discovered/would have originated
E) was discovering/had originated
3. After the flood, there was ....... damage to both crops and property.
A) favourable
B) detailed
C) irritated
D) extensive
E) incapable
4. It rained ........ throughout our holiday in Hawaii, so we came back rather frustrated.
A) exclusively
B) instantly
C) constantly
D) enormously
E) adequately
5. I don't understand the point of your learning ....... Japanese if you never expect to visit ........ country.
A) —/the
B) the/a
C) a/some
D) any/any
E) some/—
6. I've heard that our company is being ........ by a larger one. I hope we don't lose our jobs!
A) picked up
B) put off
C) taken over
D) held on
E) turned down
7. We should really set out now; ......... it will be dark when we get there.
A) otherwise
B) whereas
C) in addition to
D) thus
E) in case
8. You never believed that she would recover her stolen jewellery, .......?
A) would she
B) did you
C) wouldn't you
D) hadn't she
E) didn't you
9. The increasing availability of guns and poisons is largely responsible ...... the extermination ....... many species.
A) with/in
B) for/of
C) over/for
D) about/with
E) at/among
10. I .......... you if I .......... the meeting would take so long.
A) had phoned/might have known
B) was phoning/were to know
C) must have phoned/knew
D) would have phoned/had known
E) will be phoning/have known

A) better

C) many
D) more
E) well

A) With / by
B) For / in
C) At / along
D) By / from
E) Through / over

A) any
B) either
